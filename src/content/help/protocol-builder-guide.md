# Protocol Builder Help Guide

## What is the Protocol Builder?

The Protocol Builder helps you create safe, evidence-based treatment protocols for psychedelic-assisted therapy. It provides real-time safety checks and interaction warnings to ensure patient safety.

---

## Getting Started

### Step 1: Select Your Substance

1. Click the **"New Protocol"** button from your dashboard
2. Choose a substance from the dropdown menu (e.g., Psilocybin, MDMA, Ketamine)
3. The system will load safety data for your selected substance

**Tip:** Only substances with established safety profiles are available.

---

### Step 2: Set the Dosage

1. Enter the dosage amount in the **"Dosage"** field
2. Select the unit (mg, g, or ¬µg)
3. The system will automatically check if the dosage is within safe ranges

**Safety Check:** If your dosage exceeds recommended levels, you'll see a warning message. Review the warning carefully before proceeding.

---

### Step 3: Add Medications

1. Click **"Add Medication"** to include any medications your patient is currently taking
2. Select from the dropdown list of common medications
3. The system will check for dangerous drug interactions

**Safety Check:** Red warnings indicate contraindicated combinations (do not combine). Yellow warnings indicate caution (monitor closely).

---

### Step 4: Review Safety Checks

Before saving your protocol, review all safety warnings:

- **Drug Interactions:** Check for any red or yellow alerts
- **Dosage Validation:** Ensure dosage is within safe ranges
- **Contraindications:** Review any patient-specific warnings

**Important:** Safety checks are advisory only. Always use your clinical judgment and consult additional resources when needed.

---

### Step 5: Save Your Protocol

1. Review all information for accuracy
2. Click **"Save Protocol"** to add it to your protocols list
3. You can edit or delete protocols from the "My Protocols" page

---

### Contraindication Alerts

The system checks for conditions that may make treatment unsafe.

**Common contraindications:**
- Cardiovascular conditions (for certain substances)
- Psychiatric conditions (e.g., psychosis risk)

## Best Practices

### How to Interpret Warnings

1. **Read the full warning message** - Don't just look at the color
2. **Check the mechanism** - Understand why the interaction occurs
3. **Review recommendations** - Follow suggested actions
4. **Use clinical judgment** - Warnings are advisory, not absolute

### When to Consult Additional Resources

You should seek additional guidance when:
- Multiple red warnings appear
- You're unfamiliar with a specific interaction
- Patient has complex medical history
- Dosage falls outside typical ranges

**Resources:**
- Peer consultation
- Medical literature
- Clinical guidelines
- Pharmacology references

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I save a protocol with warnings?

Yes. The system allows you to proceed with warnings because clinical context matters. However, you must acknowledge warnings before saving.

### How often is the safety data updated?

Our drug interaction database is updated quarterly with the latest research. Receptor affinity data is based on peer-reviewed studies.

### Can I edit a protocol after saving?

Yes. Go to "My Protocols," select the protocol you want to edit, and click "Edit." All safety checks will run again when you save changes.

### What if a medication isn't in the list?

If a medication isn't available in our dropdown, you can request it be added by contacting support. For immediate needs, consult additional drug interaction resources.

### Are these safety checks a substitute for clinical judgment?

No. The Protocol Builder is a decision support tool, not a replacement for your clinical expertise. Always consider individual patient factors and consult additional resources when needed.

### Can I share protocols with other practitioners?

Yes, if you have a Partner or Pro account. Shared protocols maintain all safety checks and warnings for the receiving practitioner to review.

### What happens if I enter a very high dosage?

The system will display a prominent red warning. You can still proceed, but you must acknowledge the warning. We recommend reviewing dosage guidelines before proceeding.

### How do I know if a drug interaction is serious?

Red warnings indicate serious interactions that could cause harm. Yellow warnings indicate interactions that require monitoring. Always read the full warning message and mechanism explanation.
