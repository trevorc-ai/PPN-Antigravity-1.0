## 4. OPEN VOCABULARY STANDARD POSITIONING DOC

**Title:** Why we are open-sourcing our clinical vocabulary

**Audience:** Researchers, practitioners, policy makers

**Length:** One page

---

Clinical vocabulary for psychedelic-assisted therapy does not exist in a usable form. Practitioners across the country are documenting the same concepts with different words. Researchers cannot aggregate data across studies because the fields do not match. Payers cannot evaluate outcomes because the outcomes are not described in a shared language.

PPN Portal is building that shared vocabulary. And we are making it open.

**What open means:**

Any researcher or institution can cite the PPN vocabulary in their publications. Any practitioner can submit concepts for review through their site. Any approved addition is credited to the practitioner who originated it and visible to every site in the network.

The vocabulary is not proprietary. It belongs to the practitioners who built it.

**Why this matters:**

Proprietary vocabularies create lock-in. If your data is tagged with our terms and we close or pivot, your data becomes unreadable. Open vocabularies travel with you. Your outcomes data stays interpretable regardless of what platform you use in the future.

**How it works:**

When a practitioner types a concept that does not exist in the vocabulary yet, they can submit it as a vocabulary request. The advisory board votes on it. If 3 or more sites submit the same concept, it goes on the board agenda. At 7 sites, it fast-tracks. At 15, it gets presumptive approval pending safety review.

Your clinical judgment drives the vocabulary. We just run the process.

**The PR hook for researchers:**

The PPN vocabulary can be cited in publications as a living clinical reference. As the vocabulary grows, so does its citability. Advisory board members get co-authorship credit on any vocabulary documentation papers we publish.

## 5. SAFAR COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATION

**For internal use and advisor briefings. Not for public distribution yet.**

| Dimension | Safar | PPN Portal |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| Data structure | Voice AI generates unstructured notes | Structured fields, controlled vocabulary |
| Vocabulary | Emergent from voice transcription | Clinician-voted, advisory-governed |
| Data portability | Tied to Safar's parsing model | Open vocabulary, exportable |
| Regulatory posture | Unclear how voice notes handle documentation standards | Structured fields designed for audit-readiness |
| Research usability | Requires NLP to extract concepts from notes | Data is already coded and queryable |
| Practitioner effort | Low friction at input, high friction at analysis | Structured at input, instant at analysis |

**One-line positioning:**
Safar captures what practitioners say. PPN captures what practitioners mean.

The distinction holds because our vocabulary is defined in advance by the practitioners who actually use it, not inferred after the fact by a language model.
