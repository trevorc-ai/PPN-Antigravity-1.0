Quick Talking Points

"Documentation doesn't create legal exposure. Lack of documentation creates legal exposure. PPN builds the record structurally ‚Äî without storing what regulations protect."
If they ask about patient privacy:
"PPN has never stored a single patient name. If the database were handed to the FBI tomorrow, they'd receive encrypted codes and table references. Nothing useful."
If they ask about the Interaction Checker:
"Right now, practitioners are asking each other about drug conflicts in Signal groups. This ends that. The answer is here, sourced, and defensible."

"EHRs store patient data. PPN stores outcomes. The difference is what ends up in a subpoena versus what protects you in one."
If they ask about the training program model:
"One training program deal brings their whole cohort with them. A single partnership can mean 40 practitioners from one conversation."
