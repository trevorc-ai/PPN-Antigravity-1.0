The Pitch Flow

Step 1
The Positioning (B2B SaaS)
2 min
▾
Say this
"We are not a healthcare provider. We are a B2B SaaS analytics company. We do not employ doctors, we do not dictate care, and we do not touch the patient."
"We structured it this way deliberately to entirely bypass Corporate Practice of Medicine (CPOM) regulations. Our customers own the clinical risk."
Actions
→Show the Practitioner Dashboard. Emphasize it's a tool for independent clinics.
○
Step 2
Zero-PHI Data Moat
5 min
▾
⚡ Structural Defense
Say this
"This is our primary structural defense against HIPAA liability. We possess zero Personally Identifiable Information (PII) mapped to clinical outcomes."
"The clinic keeps the name. We only ever see 'SUBJ-9X4B-22'. We are building a massive aggregated clinical database, entirely devoid of toxic data liability."
Actions
→Navigate to the Wellness Journey. Show the subject ID entry field.
→Ask Question A: "Given we structurally bypassed HIPAA, do enterprise clinics demand BAAs anyway as a matter of policy?"
○
Step 3
The Regulatory Boundary (SaMD)
5 min
▾
Say this
"We are a 'Clinical Decision Support' tool. We visualize retrospective statistical realities. We never proactively diagnose or prescribe."
"We want to know: where is the exact line where visualizing outcome data crosses into the FDA's definition of a Software as a Medical Device?"
Actions
→Show the Global Benchmarks / Analytics heatmap.
→Ask Question B: "How do we structurally avoid SaMD classification as our heuristics get smarter?"
○
Step 4
The "Trade Secret" Strategy
3 min
▾
Say this
"We intend to protect our algorithmic models and NNT (Number Needed to Treat) calculators as Trade Secrets internally, not via defensive utility patents."
Actions
→Ask Question C: "What employment contracts or vendor NDAs do we need on Day 1 to ensure trade secrets hold up in court if an engineer defects?"
