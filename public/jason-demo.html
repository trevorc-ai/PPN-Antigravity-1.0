Objection Handling
What about patient privacy?
"We don't collect the information that HIPAA protects. No names, no SSN, no date of birth, no addresses. Nothing. The system literally cannot violate HIPAA because the regulated information never enters the system. This is a structural constraint, not a policy."
Can the government subpoena my records?
"They can try. But we don't have the information they'd be looking for. If the database were subpoenaed tomorrow, they'd receive encrypted numbers and standardized medical codes. There are no names. No session notes. Nothing that connects to a person. Structurally impossible to produce."
How is this different from Althia?
"Althia captures forms. PPN creates collective intelligence. Practitioners who've used both say Althia's form is non-user-friendly and the data sits in a silo. Our data flows into a global benchmark. Every session you record makes the system smarter for every other practitioner on the network. Different category entirely."
Is this secure?
"Built to pharmaceutical standards. Same data architecture as a clinical trial database. SNOMED and MedDRA codes throughout ‚Äî the same standards the FDA and NIH use. If Dr. Allen handed this to a review board tomorrow, nothing in the architecture would raise a flag."
What does it cost?
"14-day free trial. No credit card. After that, $99 per month for solo practitioners. Clinic plan available for multi-site operations. Start free ‚Äî no risk."
Is it ready? Can we use it now?
"It's live right now at ppnportal.net. You can create an account, enter a session, and see benchmark data tonight. We're actively onboarding early practitioners. This is the moment to be in."
We already use paper records / we have a system
"PPN doesn't replace your existing notes or workflow. It sits alongside it. You spend 60 seconds entering the anonymized data, and in return you get access to the entire network's intelligence.
