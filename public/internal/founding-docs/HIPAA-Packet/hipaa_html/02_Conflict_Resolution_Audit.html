Architecture Verification

Independent review of PPN Portal's data architecture against HIPAA Safe Harbor requirements. Each item below reflects a confirmed design decision, not an aspirational claim. Review conducted March 2026.

Privacy Architecture — Confirmed Design Decisions

AreaDesign DecisionStatus
Patient Age Age is stored as a generalized range bracket, not as an exact integer. This aligns with HIPAA Safe Harbor requirements for age-related identifiers and with PPN's published Privacy Policy. ✓ Confirmed
Session Timestamps Session timing data is handled per counsel-approved approach. Timestamps used for operational purposes (session sequencing, practitioner workflow) are retained in protected operational tables not included in de-identified outputs or analytics exports. ✓ Confirmed
Patient Identifier Design Patient codes are cryptographically random system-generated values (format PT-XXXXXXXXXX). They are not derived from any patient attribute. The plaintext code never leaves the practitioner's device — only a one-way hash is transmitted to PPN servers. Analytics and export tables store only the hash. ✓ Confirmed
Patient Weight Exact body weight is not stored. Weight is recorded as a generalized range (±5 kg bands with both kg and lbs labels). Clinically validated as appropriate for all safety monitoring use cases, March 2026. ✓ Confirmed
Free-Text Patient Inputs No clinical form accepts free-text patient identification. All inputs use structured, fixed-vocabulary controls: dropdowns, sliders, checkboxes, and foreign-key selections. No practitioner can type a patient name, identifier, or any PHI directly into a clinical record. ✓ Confirmed
