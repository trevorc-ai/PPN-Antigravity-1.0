PPN's Architecture: Zero-PHI by Design

PPN Portal is not an EHR. It is a clinical intelligence platform built from the ground up to capture zero Protected Health Information. This was not a constraint to work around. It was the founding design principle.

Every patient in PPN is assigned a cryptographically generated, synthetic Subject ID at the moment of entry. No patient names, dates of birth, addresses, or free-text clinical notes are ever entered, stored, or transmitted. This is not after-the-fact anonymization. It is a zero-knowledge architecture enforced from the first click.

Three Ways PPN Protects Your License

01
Subpoena Immunity
If a regulator, law enforcement agency, or plaintiff's attorney subpoenas the PPN database, the only data producible is aggregate anonymized metrics. Patient identity cannot be disclosed from a system that never recorded it. Your clinic cannot be compelled to produce what does not exist.
02
HIPAA Breach Liability: $0
Human error accounts for the majority of healthcare data breaches. A staff member who clicks the wrong link, a laptop left in an Uber, a phishing email. By capturing no identifying information at entry, PPN eradicates that exposure entirely. If the system were ever breached, there is nothing of clinical or legal value to take.

PHI regulations prevent standard EHRs from enabling outcome comparisons across institutions. Because PPN stores no PHI, it is the only platform in this space that can legally benchmark your clinic's outcomes against the broader practitioner network, turning your documentation into actionable business intelligence.
