## Substance-by-substance review

Psilocybin  
Core pattern: most meaningful interactions are pharmacodynamic, not pharmacokinetic, and cluster into (1) efficacy blunting/altered phenomenology (SSRIs/SNRIs, antipsychotics, benzodiazepines), (2) seizure risk signal with lithium, and (3) MAOI-related pressor/serotonin-toxicity risk where relevant. ţłÇciteţłéturn4search0ţłéturn2view2ţłéturn16search0ţłéturn13search3ţłéturn1search2ţłéturn17view0ţłéturn6search27ţłü

Efficacy blunting: a key uncertainty is that escitalopram pretreatment in a controlled crossover study did not reduce psilocybinÔÇÖs positive mood effects and reduced adverse effects, while large retrospective reports show substantial rates of diminished effects during SSRI/SNRI use and for months after discontinuation. MDMA  
Core pattern: MDMA is the most interaction-dense substance in this set because it acts as a serotonin (and norepinephrine/dopamine) releaser and transporter ligand, making it uniquely dangerous with MAOIs and other serotonergic drugs. ## Medication-class review

SSRIs  
Medical danger: highest danger is not with psilocybin/LSD directly, but with MAOI contexts (ayahuasca/harmalas) and with MDMA (because MDMA increases synaptic serotonin and SSRIs can add serotonergic load and alter PK). ţłÇciteţłéturn17view0ţłéturn11search3ţłéturn6search3ţłéturn0search2ţłü

## Conflicts, ambiguities, and evidence gaps

Serotonin syndrome risk is frequently overstated for classic psychedelics in isolation, and the literature warns that diagnosis can be confounded by overlapping toxidromes and variable criteria. Your platform should treat serotonin toxicity as a spectrum and should reserve ÔÇťhigh-risk serotonin syndromeÔÇŁ flags for combinations that clearly increase synaptic serotonin (MAOI plus serotonergic agent; serotonin releaser plus serotonergic agent), with MDMA plus MAOI at the top. ţłÇciteţłéturn5search2ţłéturn6search29ţłéturn5search30ţłéturn6search1ţłéturn17view0ţłü  

Psilocybin plus SSRIs/SNRIs is the most important ÔÇťmixed evidenceÔÇŁ area for clinical workflows. Controlled escitalopram pretreatment data show no meaningful reduction in positive mood effects and reduced adverse effects, while large retrospective survey data suggest substantial subjective weakening and persistence after discontinuation. This could be driven by differences in exposure duration (2 weeks vs chronic), medication type/dose, selection biases, or expectancy effects. Your checker should not collapse this into a single deterministic rule. ţłÇciteţłéturn4search0ţłéturn2view2ţłéturn4search10ţłéturn9search31ţłü  

Benzodiazepines are another area where simplistic rules will be wrong. Ketamine has a clear toxicity interaction with benzodiazepines (sedation/respiratory depression) and a debated efficacy interaction (possible attenuation of antidepressant response at higher benzodiazepine doses). For psilocybin, controlled coadministration with midazolam suggests the acute psychedelic experience can still occur while memory and downstream meaning-making may change, meaning that ÔÇťbenzodiazepines block psychedelicsÔÇŁ is not a defensible universal claim. ţłÇciteţłéturn19view0ţłéturn4search8ţłéturn4search3ţłéturn8view1ţłü  

Ibogaine interaction evidence is dominated by non-randomized and case-report data. Despite that, the QT signal is strong enough that it justifies conservative contraindication logic with other QT-prolonging agents. The evidence gap is not whether QT prolongation is a risk, but how to quantify individualized risk and what minimum monitoring protocols reliably mitigate it. ţłÇciteţłéturn11search6ţłéturn0search7ţłéturn0search23ţłéturn11search12ţłéturn11search8ţłü  

5ÔÇĹMeOÔÇĹDMT and ayahuasca interactions are complicated by variable composition, unregulated preparations, and uncertain dose. Mechanistic MAOI logic is strong, but incidence and dose-response are not well quantified. Your platform should favor ingredient-level screening (harmalas present or not, serotonergic co-meds present or not) and should explicitly label evidence strength as ÔÇťcase-report and mechanisticÔÇŁ when that is the basis. ţłÇciteţłéturn11search3ţłéturn11search15ţłéturn5search3ţłéturn11search23ţłü  

Mescaline-specific DDIs are relatively under-characterized. Most caution is extrapolated from phenethylamine physiology and MAOI contraindication frameworks rather than from direct studies. Overconfident automation here risks hallucinating certainty. ţłÇciteţłéturn17view0ţłéturn6search0ţłü

Structured data schema recommendation  
A practical schema for a clinical-grade interaction knowledge base should separate ÔÇťinteraction claimÔÇŁ from ÔÇťevidence objectsÔÇŁ and should allow multiple evidence items per claim with explicit provenance. The minimum viable design:

Interaction table (one row per psychedelicÔÇôingredient pair, not merely class):

Why this structure is necessary:
You need to represent conflicting evidence without overwriting. Final recommendation  
What should be automated with hard stops:
MAOI detection (including linezolid/methylene blue and harmala exposure) combined with MDMA, ayahuasca/harmalas, or 5ÔÇĹMeOÔÇĹDMT; ibogaine combined with QTÔÇĹprolonging agents or strong metabolic inhibitors; ketamine/esketamine combined with major CNS depressants. These are the combinations where the cost of a false negative is unacceptably high and where evidence or label-grade warnings exist. ţłÇciteţłéturn17view0ţłéturn8view2ţłéturn5search3ţłéturn11search6ţłéturn19view0ţłéturn18view0ţłü  

What should be automated as ÔÇťstrong caution / clinician reviewÔÇŁ rather than as contraindication:
SSRI/SNRI co-use with psilocybin and LSD (because evidence supports blunting but does not support a universal danger claim); stimulant co-use with classic psychedelics (because risk is context- and comorbidity-dependent); anticoagulants/antiplatelets and many endocrine meds (because risk is mostly indirect and evidence is limited). The platform should show the rationale and uncertainty, not pretend precision. ţłÇciteţłéturn4search0ţłéturn2view2ţłéturn13search10ţłéturn7search31ţłéturn18view0ţłéturn17view0ţłü  

What should not be fully automated:
Dose-based safety, individualized QT risk scoring without ECG data, and psychiatric destabilization prediction. The evidence base and individual variability are too large. These should be ÔÇťdecision-support promptsÔÇŁ with structured documentation fields (history of arrhythmia, baseline QTc, bipolar/psychosis history, recent medication changes), not deterministic outputs. ţłÇciteţłéturn11search6ţłéturn7search0ţłéturn7search16ţłéturn6search29ţłü
