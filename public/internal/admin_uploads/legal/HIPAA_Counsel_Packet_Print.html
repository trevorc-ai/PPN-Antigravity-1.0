1. Executive Summary and Purpose

PPN Portal is a cloud-based structured clinical documentation, outcomes tracking, and benchmarking platform for licensed psychedelic therapy practitioners and clinical programs. This packet is submitted to obtain legal counsel's independent analysis of whether PPN Portal creates, receives, maintains, or transmits Protected Health Information on behalf of Covered Entities under HIPAA.

This packet does not state final legal conclusions. PPN Portal requests counsel's analysis of the facts and assumptions described below.

2. Material Assumptions for Counsel's Review

  • PPN Portal is a SaaS platform for licensed practitioners and clinical facilities for structured documentation, longitudinal outcomes tracking, quality improvement, safety surveillance, and aggregate benchmarking.
  • PPN Portal is not intended to function as an EHR, billing platform, claims processor, or healthcare clearinghouse.
  • Direct patient identity is intended to remain outside the in-scope PPN clinical data model.
  • Patient continuity relies on a two-layer pseudonymous subject-linking model: internal subject_id and practitioner-facing PT-XXXXXX display code.
  • The current platform retains certain date-related fields, including session_date. The legal significance of these fields is presented as an open issue for counsel.
  • This memorandum addresses the current implemented fact pattern and does not rely on deprecated architecture or future work not yet completed.

3. Covered Entity Analysis

PPN Portal does not believe it is a Covered Entity, but requests counsel's independent analysis. Based on current understanding:

  • Health plan: PPN Portal is not a health plan. It does not provide or pay for medical care.
  • Healthcare clearinghouse: PPN Portal does not process HIPAA standard transactions.
  • Healthcare provider: PPN Portal does not diagnose, treat, or prescribe. It is a software platform used by providers.

Counsel is asked to confirm whether PPN Portal should be analyzed as outside the Covered Entity definition under the facts described in this packet.

4. Business Associate Analysis

PPN Portal understands that the more important question is whether it is a Business Associate with respect to customers who are themselves Covered Entities. PPN Portal requests counsel's analysis of whether the platform's intended data model, two-layer pseudonymous subject-linking approach, operational boundaries, and supporting vendor stack are sufficient to avoid Business Associate status.

Counsel Focus: PPN Portal is not asking counsel to assume the answer. PPN Portal is asking counsel to evaluate the actual implemented fact pattern.

5. Data Elements Intended to Enter the Platform

  1. Internal canonical subject identifier: subject_id (BIGINT, system-generated)
  2. Practitioner-facing pseudonymous display code: PT-XXXXXX (6-character alphanumeric)
  3. Structured clinical codes and controlled-vocabulary references
  4. Treatment-related structured values (substance, dosage, route)
  5. Vital sign values and structured safety fields
  6. Outcome scores and assessment values (PHQ-9, GAD-7, MEQ-30, etc.)
  7. Session sequence and structured workflow values
  8. Date-related fields including session_date — open legal issue
  9. Practitioner account information for authentication and access
  10. Site and organization identifiers for access scoping
  11. Subscription and payment information handled through Stripe

6. Data Elements Intended to Be Excluded

The platform is intended not to store in the in-scope clinical schema the following direct patient identifiers:

  • Patient legal name
  • Patient date of birth
  • Patient street address
  • Patient phone number
  • Patient email address
  • Social Security Number
  • Insurance identifiers
  • Medical record number
  • Free-text narrative patient identity fields
  • Patient photographs in ordinary clinical workflows

These exclusions are intended to be architectural constraints, not policy preferences. Counsel is asked to assess whether the exclusions, if accurate in production, are sufficient to support the legal conclusions sought.

7. Date-Related Fields — Open Legal Issue

Specific Counsel Analysis Requested: Although the platform is intended to exclude direct patient identity, the current application retains session_date and other temporal fields. PPN Portal is not asking counsel to assume this has already been resolved.

Counsel is asked to analyze whether:

  1. Retention of session_date prevents reliance on Safe Harbor
  2. Date-related fields require Expert Determination or narrower use-boundary analysis
  3. Date-related fields may remain acceptable for internal use while requiring different treatment in exports
  4. Additional suppression, generalization, or year-only transformation is required

8. Two-Layer Pseudonymous Subject-Linking Architecture

Layer 1 — Internal Canonical Database Identifier

The internal canonical identifier is subject_id. It is a system-generated BIGINT value used as the database-level primary key for patient-level linkage across sessions and related clinical records.

  • Generated by the system; not derived from any patient attribute
  • Not intended to encode patient-identifying information
  • Not ordinarily exposed to practitioners in the UI

Layer 2 — Practitioner-Facing Display Code

The practitioner-facing display code uses the format PT-XXXXXX, where the suffix is a 6-character alphanumeric value generated client-side at patient creation.

  • Contains no patient-derived information
  • Not intended to encode any direct patient identifier
  • Functions as the practitioner-facing pseudonymous patient reference in the UI

Identity Boundary

The practitioner holds the only mapping between the display code and the real patient identity. PPN Portal stores the internal canonical identifier and the display code, but does not use patient name, DOB, MRN, or other direct patient identity as the patient-linking value in the clinical data model.

Canonical Statement for All Legal Materials: PPN Portal uses a two-layer patient-linking model consisting of an internal system-generated database identifier (subject_id) and a practitioner-facing pseudonymous display code in the format PT-XXXXXX. The internal identifier is the canonical database linkage key. The display code is the practitioner-visible reference used in the UI. Neither identifier is derived from patient name, date of birth, or other direct patient identity.

9. Safe Harbor Analysis Summary

PPN Portal is not asking counsel to assume that all 18 Safe Harbor categories have already been fully satisfied. The narrower position is:

  1. Direct patient identity fields appear intentionally excluded from the in-scope clinical schema
  2. Date-related fields and patient-linking identifiers require legal confirmation against the actual implemented fact pattern
  3. Counsel should evaluate whether the current design supports Safe Harbor, whether Expert Determination is more appropriate, or whether PPN should narrow its claims pending further changes

Until counsel review is complete, the public-facing posture uses language such as:

  • "Designed to avoid storing direct patient identifiers"
  • "Built around pseudonymized structured clinical data"
  • "Formal legal review is in progress"

The following language has been removed or is being removed from public materials:

  • "HIPAA does not apply"
  • "All 18 Safe Harbor identifiers are confirmed compliant"
  • "No BAA is required" as a final categorical statement
  • "Zero-PHI architecture confirmed"
  • "Immunity to Subpoenas"

12. Specific Questions for Counsel

  1. Based on the facts described, is PPN Portal a HIPAA Covered Entity?
  2. Based on the facts described, is PPN Portal a HIPAA Business Associate?
  3. Do the records stored in the in-scope environment constitute PHI, individually or in combination?
  4. Does the two-layer subject-linking architecture support de-identification under Safe Harbor, Expert Determination, or neither?
  5. Do retained date-related fields, including session_date, prevent reliance on Safe Harbor?
  6. Do operational logs, vendor systems, backups, or metadata create PHI risk even if the clinical schema excludes direct patient identifiers?
  7. Is a Business Associate Agreement required for any current workflow?
  8. Are any current public claims too strong relative to the current implemented architecture?
  9. What additional materials are needed before counsel could issue a reliable written opinion?

Appendix D — Canonical Subject Identifier Workflow

Layer 1 — Internal canonical identifier: subject_id (BIGINT). System-generated. Not derived from patient name, DOB, MRN, or any other patient attribute. Not ordinarily exposed in the UI.

Layer 2 — Practitioner-facing display code: PT-XXXXXX (6-character alphanumeric). Generated client-side at patient creation. Shown in the UI as the ordinary patient reference. Contains no patient-derived information.

Identity boundary: The practitioner holds the only mapping between the display code and the real patient identity. PPN Portal stores neither a patient's legal name nor any other direct patient identity as the patient-linking value in the clinical data model.

What the platform stores: subject_id (canonical), PT-XXXXXX (display alias), and structured clinical records linked through the internal canonical identifier.
