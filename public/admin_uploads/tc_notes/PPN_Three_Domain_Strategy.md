## Why One Brand Across All Three

The alternative would be to create separate brands for each layer â€”
a different name, logo, and website for each. This is how most
companies approach it. The problem is:

- It confuses practitioners who encounter the brand in different contexts
- It divides marketing investment across three separate identities
- It makes it harder for press and partners to understand the big picture

By keeping all three under the PPN Portal name, every piece of press
coverage, every conference badge, every word-of-mouth referral builds
the same brand equity. When someone hears "PPN Portal" in a supervision
circle, and then sees "ppnportal.org" cited in a journal article,
and then searches "ppnportal.com" â€” it all reinforces the same
singular identity.

**One name. Three rooms. One campus.**
